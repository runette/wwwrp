chevron_right About This App

Background

The Sandwich Flats Foreshore Project is an initiative by a group of organisations, including the Nautical Archeology Society, MSDS Marine and CITiZAN.

The intention is to create better understanding about the wrecks on the Sandwich Flats foreshore and for local people and members to gain experience of techniques such as

  • Walk-over survey
  • 2D measured tape survey
  • Total station survey of wreck sites
  • Wreck conservation assessments
  • Photogrammetry
  • Site Recorder practice
  • Use of the CITiZAN app to record coastal features

    • This app is an independent development based with permission on the outputs from the Project.

    How to use the App Basics

    The app is a map. You can zoom and move the app to where you want OR you can use the button to sense your device location and move to that location.

    The map will actually work anywhere in the UK but the site data is only displayed for the Sandwich Flats area.

    As you zoom into the Sandwich Flats area, more details will be shown down to the level of individual finds and timbers where they have been recorded.

    Best Practice

    The intended use for the app is for you to turn on automatic location using the button and, as you walk around the site, use the map to identify what you can see on the ground.

    Legend and Information

    The map legend can be shown by select the map menu in the tool bar.

    The map Site Inventory can be shown by select the list_alt menu in the tool bar.

    Some markers on the map, specifically wrecks either identified by a red circle (i.e. sites in the Site Inventory) or a blue triangle (offshore wrecks in the UKHO data) have more information. Click or tap on the marker to see the information pop up.

    For markers in the Site Inventory, click or tap on the launch icon to go directly to the inventory entry.

         Installing the App

    This web app is a special type of application (known as a Progressive Web Application) that works as either a website or an App on your phone.

    If you using a modern browser (i.e. latest versions of Chrome, Edge, Firefox or Safari), you can add the app to your homescreen. This puts an icon for the App on your device that works a lot like a normal app without taking very much memory or other resources.This will make the app faster and work better with no connection

    NOTE that this is a map based app and like other such apps including Google Maps, the map is not really available when there is no connection and so may not display correctly, or at all, without a connection.

    How you do this does depend a lot on the browser and device. Using Chrome or Edge on Android or desktop, you may see a banner asking you to install the app or you can go to the menu where you will see an item that says "Install Sandwidh Bay Flats Project" or "Add to Home Screen". There is also a small plus sign next to the facvourite button that you can press.

    On the iPhone, select 'Share" and look for "add to Home Screen"

     Advanced Features

    The app is currently testing one advanced, experimental feature that will work only with the latest versions of Chrome and Edge.

    This feature will over ride the screen saver function of the phone if, and only if, you flip the "Wake Lock" switch on the settings tab.

    This means that the app will stay active and visible for as long as you want. This also means that the phone will use much more power and run out much more quickly.

    Note that this over ride only lasts as long as the App is on the screen. If you switch away from the app or minimize the app, the normal screen saver functions return.
